# Baholo Projects hero video prompt for Google Flow / Omni

## Recommended setup

- Upload `assets/images/hero-welder.webp` as the only visual reference and starting frame.
- Format: 16:9 landscape, 4K if available, 24 fps.
- Duration: 10-12 seconds.
- Camera: one continuous, very slow push-in. Avoid a montage; a single controlled shot loops better and produces fewer visual errors.
- Website use: muted autoplay, looped, with the uploaded WebP retained as the poster and mobile fallback.

## Main prompt

Create a 10-12 second photorealistic cinematic documentary video for the homepage of Baholo Projects, a South African industrial supply, property maintenance, welding and fabrication company.

Use the uploaded welding workshop image as the strict visual anchor. Preserve the same single welder, fully lowered dark welding helmet, charcoal protective workwear with restrained orange piping, heavy gloves, MIG welding torch, rectangular steel frame, clamps, workshop architecture, daylight direction and overall composition. Do not redesign the worker, clothing, protective equipment, steel frame or setting.

The worker remains bent over the steel frame in the centre-right of the composition and performs one continuous, controlled weld along the same joint. Movement must be restrained and mechanically credible: the torch advances slowly by a few centimetres, the gloved support hand remains stable, the shoulders shift subtly with the weld, and a small number of natural sparks fall downward and extinguish quickly. The steel frame, clamps and background racks remain completely stable. Ambient daylight at the open workshop door changes only imperceptibly.

The welding helmet must remain fully lowered and opaque for the entire video. The worker must never lift, tilt back or remove the helmet, turn toward the camera, reveal any facial feature, or expose the eyes through the visor. The person remains anonymous throughout.

Keep the left 40 percent of the frame calm and compositionally stable for white website headline text. No person, sparks, moving equipment or new object may enter this copy-safe area. Use one slow, stable dolly-in of approximately four percent with subtle natural handheld micro-movement. Do not orbit around the worker or change to an angle that could reveal the face. Maintain realistic depth, correct perspective and perfectly consistent object geometry throughout.

Lighting is directional late-afternoon daylight mixed with neutral practical workshop lighting. Preserve natural skin texture, fabric creases, scuffed steel, workshop dust and subtle 35 mm film grain. Use neutral steel-grey and black tones with restrained orange details. The result should feel like high-end commercial documentary footage captured on location, not a staged stock video or visual-effects sequence.

Make the final frame composition, helmet position, torch position and worker silhouette closely resemble the opening frame so the video can loop without a visible jump. No audio is required.

## Negative constraints

No logos, captions, titles, signage, readable text or watermarks. No visible face, eyes, skin through the visor or facial reflection. Never raise, open, remove or make the welding helmet transparent. No head turn toward camera. No identity drift. No extra people entering the foreground. No extra fingers or limbs. No warped hands, torch, steel sections, clamps, cables or tools. No duplicated equipment. No changing frame dimensions. No impossible machinery movement. No exploding sparks, dense smoke, steam, lens flares or floating dust effects. No heroic posing. No teal-and-orange blockbuster grade. No speed ramp, orbit, drone move, whip pan, jump cut, time-lapse or camera shake. Do not brighten or clutter the left-side copy area.

## Acceptance check

Reject any result where:

- the helmet lifts, opens, turns toward camera or reveals any part of the face;
- the worker, protective clothing or workshop changes between frames;
- hands, torch, clamps or steel frame deform;
- the left copy-safe area becomes busy or bright;
- sparks dominate the scene;
- the ending cannot loop cleanly into the opening;
- the footage looks glossy, staged, synthetic or overly cinematic.
