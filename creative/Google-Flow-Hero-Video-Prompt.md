# Baholo Projects hero video prompt for Google Flow

## Recommended setup

- Upload `assets/images/hero-workshop.webp` as the visual reference or starting frame.
- Format: 16:9 landscape, 4K if available, 24 fps.
- Duration: 10-12 seconds.
- Camera: one continuous, very slow push-in. Avoid a montage; a single controlled shot loops better and produces fewer visual errors.
- Website use: muted autoplay, looped, with the uploaded WebP retained as the poster and mobile fallback.

## Main prompt

Create a 10-12 second photorealistic cinematic documentary video for the homepage of Baholo Projects, a South African industrial supply, property maintenance, welding and fabrication company.

Use the uploaded workshop image as the strict visual anchor. Preserve the same two Black South African professionals, their faces, hairstyles, body proportions, charcoal workwear with restrained orange details, the industrial valve assembly, workshop architecture, lighting direction and overall composition. Do not redesign the people, clothing, equipment or setting.

The scene takes place in a real, functioning medium-sized South African fabrication workshop. The two professionals remain in the right half of the frame, naturally reviewing a technical drawing and checking the industrial valve together. Their movements are small and credible: one person steadies the drawing while the other traces a measurement, they briefly exchange a focused glance, and the paper moves slightly in the workshop air. A background welder performs one short controlled weld with only a few natural sparks. Workshop lights flicker imperceptibly and daylight shifts subtly through the high windows.

Keep the left 42 percent of the frame darker, calm and visually uncluttered for white website headline text. Nothing important may enter this copy-safe area. Use a slow, stable dolly-in of approximately five percent with subtle natural handheld micro-movement, no orbit, no rapid pan and no dramatic focus pull. Maintain realistic depth, correct perspective and consistent object geometry throughout.

Lighting is directional late-afternoon daylight mixed with neutral practical workshop lighting. Preserve natural skin texture, fabric creases, scuffed steel, workshop dust and subtle 35 mm film grain. Use neutral steel-grey and black tones with restrained orange details. The result should feel like high-end commercial documentary footage captured on location, not a staged stock video or visual-effects sequence.

Make the final frame composition and subject positions closely resemble the opening frame so the video can loop without a visible jump. No audio is required.

## Negative constraints

No logos, captions, titles, signage, readable text or watermarks. No identity drift. No face morphing. No extra people entering the foreground. No extra fingers or limbs. No warped hands, paper, valves, pipes or tools. No duplicated equipment. No impossible machinery movement. No exploding sparks, dense smoke, steam, lens flares or floating dust effects. No plastic skin, beauty retouching, exaggerated smiles or heroic posing. No teal-and-orange blockbuster grade. No speed ramp, drone move, whip pan, jump cut, time-lapse or camera shake. Do not brighten or clutter the left-side copy area.

## Acceptance check

Reject any result where:

- either person's identity or clothing changes between frames;
- hands, the drawing or industrial valve deform;
- the left copy-safe area becomes busy or bright;
- sparks dominate the scene;
- the ending cannot loop cleanly into the opening;
- the footage looks glossy, staged, synthetic or overly cinematic.
